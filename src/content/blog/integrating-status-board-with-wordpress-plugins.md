Recently, Panic (The software company behind popular OSX Apps Coda and Transmit), released an iPad app called [Status Board](http://panic.com/statusboard/). I don't, myself, own an iPad but I know a lot of people who do. I also can't turn down the opportunity to build something useful that integrates multiple apps, plugins, and data points. Seriously, I think I need help.

I use [Jetpack](http://wordpress.org/extend/plugins/jetpack/) and [Easy Digital Downloads](https://easydigitaldownloads.com?ref=371) daily, and know both of them have very 'statistical' driven data sets, so I figured, "What the heck. Why not build some Status Board graphs for the people who use these plugins and own an iPad!"

Enter, **[EDD - Status Board](http://wordpress.org/extend/plugins/edd-status-board/)** and **[Jetpack Status Board](http://wordpress.org/extend/plugins/jetpack-status-board/)**. These two plugins for WordPress will give you the ability to integrate Easy Digital Downloads' Sales & Earnings and Jetpack daily views into a beautiful Status Board bar graph.

> It is integrations like this that validate the entire idea behind building the Easy Digital Downloads jSON API.
> 
> Being able to quickly glance up and see my store's stats on my iPad display is just awesome.
> 
> Huge props go to Panic for building Status Board, and a big thank you to Chris for taking the time to build the integration.
> 
> \- Pippin Williamson ([mordauk](http://profiles.wordpress.org/mordauk))  

A big thanks to [Pippin Williamson](http://pippinsplugins.com?ref=67) and [Curtis McHale](http://curtismchale.ca/) for helping me test out the early versions of these plugins.

If you do happen to try these Status Board plugins out, and find a bug, please report it on the WordPress.org support forums for the plugin, this will help consolidate any bugs you find into one place.
